Previously Asked TAG Test Coding Questions

Q1: Constellation (Star Pattern Recognition)

Problem Statement: Three characters { #, *, . } represents a constellation of stars and galaxies in space. Each galaxy is demarcated by # characters. There can be one or many stars in a given galaxy. Stars can only be in the shape of vowels { A, E, I, O, U }. A collection of * in the shape of the vowels is a star contained in a 3√ó3 block.

Input: First line: N (number of columns). Next 3 lines: Matrix with characters.

Output: Vowels (stars) in order of their occurrence within galaxies.

Input:
18
* . * # * * * # * * * # * * * . * .
* . * # * . * # . * . # * * * * * *
* * * # * * * # * * * # * * * * . *

Output:
U#O#I#EA
Q2: Minimum Gifts Distribution

Problem Statement: A company wants to distribute gifts to employees based on their ranks. Rules: 1. Each employee must receive at least one gift. 2. Employees with higher rank than neighbors must get more gifts than those neighbors. Find minimum gifts required.

Input:
2
5
1 2 1 5 2
2
1 2

Output:
7
3
Q3: Prime Hours Equivalence

Problem Statement: In a custom day system with D hours divided into P parts, find equivalent prime hours. Hour X in part 1 is equivalent to hour (X + D/P) in part 2. Count instances where both equivalent hours are prime.

Input: 24 2
Output: 3
(Pairs: 5~17, 7~19, 11~23)
Q4: Minimize Sum After Operations

Problem Statement: Given an array and K operations, minimize array sum where each operation replaces arr[i] with floor(arr[i]/2). Maximize reduction using best K choices.

Input:
4 3
20 7 5 4

Output:
17
Q5: Railway Station - Minimum Platforms

Problem Statement: Given train arrival and stoppage times, find minimum platforms needed. If Train A departs at time X and Train B arrives at X, they can't use same platform.

Input:
3
10 2
5 10
13 5

Output:
2

Problem Statement: Element X is "happy" if there exists at least one other element Y in range [X-K, X+K]. Count total happy elements.

Important Tips for TAG Test

Preparation Strategy

  • Practice on Actual Platforms: Use CodeChef and Unstop for official-style problems.
  • Time Management: Solve easy question (45 min), then medium (45 min).
  • Edge Cases: Always validate input and test boundary conditions.
  • Output Format: Match exact format - extra spaces/messages cause failures.

Common Question Types

  • Array Problems: Subarrays, maximum/minimum, counting
  • String Manipulation: Character operations, pattern matching
  • Dynamic Programming: Knapsack, subset sum, coins
  • Graph: DFS, BFS, connectivity, bridges
  • Math: Combinatorics, primes, series

Additional Guidelines

  • Languages Allowed: C, C++, Java, Python, Perl. (Eclipse-based compiler in exam).
  • No Negative Marking: Attempt all problems; partial solutions give points.
  • Differences from CodeVita Rounds: No MCQs, higher constraints, harder DSA problems, no debugging tools.
